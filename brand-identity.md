# The Bunchy Tops: Social Media Brand Identity Guide

## 1. BRAND EMOTION STATEMENT
**The warm, hazy exhale of a late-summer dusk—where the salt is still in your hair, the bass is humming in your chest, and the noise of the world fades into the sound of evening crickets.**

> **Traces back to:** The literal foley of crickets at the end of *Castaway*, the muddy, washed-out low-end warmth of *North Side*, and the overarching lyrical themes of intentional escapism and anti-conformity. The brand is not a high-energy beach party; it is the grounded, slightly nostalgic comedown when the day is finally yours.

## 3. VISUAL IDENTITY

### 3.1 Color Palette
> **Traces back to:** The blend of bright island energy (Major 7th chords) tempered by east coast grit (lo-fi production, minor key transitions). 

| Role | Color Name | Hex Code | Rationale (Musical Evidence) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary** | Deep Swell | `#1C3738` | A deep, muted ocean green/blue. Represents the heavy, warm, foundational bassline that anchors both tracks. |
| **Secondary** | Sunbaked Clay | `#C16E4F` | A warm terracotta. Reflects the analog warmth and acoustic guitar strumming in *Castaway*. |
| **Accent 1** | Golden Hour | `#DCA74E` | A muted, nostalgic yellow. Represents the bright, shimmering chorus effect on the lead guitars. |
| **Accent 2** | Wistful Blue | `#7B9095` | A faded, hazy sky blue. Ties directly to the "washed out," echo-heavy vocal production of *North Side*. |
| **Neutral 1** | Canvas/Sand | `#EAE3D8` | A textured off-white. Represents the dry, organic drum kit and shaker subdivisions. |
| **Neutral 2** | Driftwood | `#8A8175` | A warm grey. Anchors the palette with the "gritty" indie-rock edge. |
| **Dark/Text** | Charcoal | `#2B2B2B` | Never use pure black. This represents the nightfall/crickets at the end of *Castaway*. |

**Usage Rules:** 
*   **NEVER** use neon or hyper-saturated tropical colors (no bright cyan or highlighter yellow). The music is too sophisticated and nostalgic for typical "tourist Hawaii" colors.

### 3.2 Typography
> **Traces back to:** The "loose but tight" rhythm section. The fonts must feel structured but inherently relaxed and slightly retro.

*   **Headline Font:** *Recoleta* (or fallback: *Cooper Black* / *Garamond* in lowercase).
    *   *Usage:* Use for album art, video titles, and major announcements. Keep tracking (letter spacing) tight.
    *   *Why:* The rounded serifs evoke the 1970s surf/skate culture, perfectly matching the bossa-nova and surf-pop influences in the music.
*   **Body/Accent Font:** *Space Grotesk* (or fallback: *Courier New*).
    *   *Usage:* Use for lyrics, gig dates, and secondary text. Always uppercase with wide tracking.
    *   *Why:* It provides a typewriter-esque, DIY/indie zine feel, matching the "bedroom pop/lo-fi" production tier of the band.

### 3.3 Photography & Video Direction
> **Traces back to:** The "hazy, sun-drenched, nostalgic" vibe and the acoustic, unpolished instrumentation.

*   **Shooting Principles:** Shoot primarily on film or with vintage lenses (or emulate this). Seek out lens flares, natural shadows, and imperfect framing. Camera movement should be handheld but smooth—swaying, not shaking (like the 100 BPM groove).
*   **Subject Matter Hierarchy:**
    1.  The environment (waves, trees, empty roads) interacting with the band.
    2.  Instruments in natural settings (an acoustic guitar leaning on a truck).
    3.  Candid, unposed moments of the band hanging out.
    4.  *(Least)* Standard, posed "band against a brick wall" photos.
*   **Specific Shots to Seek Out:** Silhouettes against the sunset; close-ups of hands on the fretboard; smoke drifting in the air; feet in the sand or on a skateboard.
*   **Hard Avoids:** Ring lights, stark studio backdrops, hyper-crisp 4K drone footage, looking directly into the camera with "cool" poses.

### 3.4 Filter & Color Grading
> **Traces back to:** The heavy use of time-based effects (hall reverb, slapback delay) that give the music a "psychedelic, echoey quality."

*   **Photo Editing:** Emulate Kodak Portra 400. Lift the blacks (make shadows slightly grey/faded), warm up the highlights (add yellow/orange), and add a moderate amount of grain.
*   **Video Grading:** Lower the contrast. Videos should feel like a memory. Bloom the highlights slightly so the sun looks soft and glowing, not harsh.
*   **Practical Note:** Create a single Lightroom/CapCut preset based on these rules and apply it to *every* visual piece of content for instantaneous brand recognition.

## 4. VERBAL IDENTITY

### 4.1 Tone of Voice
**The voice of a close friend sitting next to you on the tailgate of a truck after the sun has gone down, talking about life over a slow acoustic jam.**
> **Traces back to:** The conversational, mid-range vocal delivery in *Castaway* and the repetitive, hypnotic, chant-like lyrics of *North Side*.

1.  **Grounded:** Rooted in physical reality. We talk about the weather, the water, and the dirt.
2.  **Unrushed:** Sentences are paced rhythmically. No exclamation-point-heavy hype.
3.  **Reflective:** We acknowledge the grind of society, but choose to look past it.
4.  **Inclusive:** "We" and "Us" rather than "I" and "Me." It's a collective experience.
5.  **Subtly Gritty:** Honest and slightly rough around the edges. We don't pretend life is a perfect vacation.

### 4.2 Vocabulary Guide
> **Traces back to:** The thematic rejection of societal constraints and the blend of island/east coast identities.

| TO USE | WHY | TO AVOID | WHY |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Drift, fade, groove, roots | Matches the mid-tempo (86-100 BPM) sway of the music. | Hustle, grind, epic, banger | Too aggressive; breaks the escapist, chillwave illusion. |
| Off the grid, sanctuary, out here | Enforces the "Castaway" theme of intentional disconnection. | Content, followers, algorithm | Peeks behind the curtain; ruins the authentic indie credibility. |
| Dusk, twilight, amber | Evokes the specific lighting and foley (crickets) of the tracks. | Party, rager, lit | Too high-energy. This is stoner/surf rock, not club music. |

### 4.3 Caption Style
> **Traces back to:** The lyrical simplicity and narrow vocal range of *North Side*—hypnotic and straightforward.

*   **Rules:** Short sentences. Let the visual and the audio do the heavy lifting. Lowercase letters are encouraged for an indie, unpretentious feel. Lead with an observation, end with an invitation.

**5 On-Brand Captions:**
1.  "sun went down. amps stayed on. new jam in the bio."
2.  "nowhere to be and all day to get there."
3.  "east coast roots, maui dirt. playing the north shore tonight at 8."
4.  "some songs are just an excuse to turn your phone off for four minutes. 'castaway' is out now."
5.  "caught a groove on the porch and let it run. what are you listening to tonight?"

**5 Off-Brand Captions (Cautionary):**
1.  "🚨 NEW MUSIC ALERT!! 🚨 Go stream our new banger Castaway right now!! Link in bio 🔥🔥🔥" *(Too loud, too desperate).*
2.  "We are so incredibly humbled and blessed to announce our new single..." *(Too formal and cliché).*
3.  "Who's ready to party this weekend?! 🍻" *(Wrong energy level).*
4.  "Please pre-save our song, it really helps the Spotify algorithm!" *(Kills the escapist vibe).*
5.  "Just a couple of guys being dudes! 🤪" *(Too goofy; undermines the musical sophistication).*

### 4.4 Emoji Policy
> **Traces back to:** The organic, nature-driven sound design (crickets, acoustic guitars).
*   **Rules:** Maximum of 1-2 emojis per post. Always placed at the very end of the caption, never in the middle of sentences.
*   **Approved Emojis:** 🌊, 🌅, 🍂, 🚐, 🌴, 🌙, 🕰️, 📻
*   **Banned Emojis:** 🔥, 💯, 🚨, 🚀, 🤪, 🤑

## 5. CONTENT PILLARS

### Pillar 1: The Sonic Landscape (40%)
*   **Description:** Raw, in-the-room musical performances. Showcasing the musicianship, specifically the interplay between the bass and the acoustic guitar.
*   **Traces back to:** The A&R note praising the "loose but tight" rhythm section and the "organic, live band in a room" production.
*   **Post Ideas:**
    1.  A Reel of the bassist and guitarist jamming the *Castaway* groove on a porch at sunset.
    2.  A carousel breaking down the bossa-nova jazz chords used in the songs (appeals to the indie/musician niche).
    3.  A lo-fi, tape-deck recorded snippet of a new song idea.

### Pillar 2: The Geography & Roots (30%)
*   **Description:** Visuals that ground the band in their environment—Maui landscapes paired with subtle nods to their east coast grit.
*   **Traces back to:** The song *North Side* and the band's core identity of place-rooted, nostalgic storytelling.
*   **Post Ideas:**
    1.  A cinematic, slow-motion Reel of waves rolling in on the North Shore, set to the hazy intro of *North Side*.
    2.  A photo dump of the band's favorite local, off-the-beaten-path spots in Maui (no tourist traps).
    3.  A short video telling the story of how Charlie Ward brought his east coast songwriting to the island.

### Pillar 3: The Unplugged Philosophy (30%)
*   **Description:** Lifestyle content that visually represents the feeling of being a "castaway"—rejecting the rush and embracing slow living.
*   **Traces back to:** The lyrical themes of personal freedom, rejecting societal constraints, and the escapist mood of the music.
*   **Post Ideas:**
    1.  A video of a campfire burning down to embers with just the ambient sound, transitioning into the cricket outro of *Castaway*.
    2.  A carousel of 35mm film photos showing a day off: surfing, reading, drinking coffee, no phones in sight.
    3.  A quote graphic (using Recoleta font on a Canvas background) with a lyric about leaving the noise behind.

## 6. PLATFORM STRATEGY

> **Traces back to:** The target demographic (16-35, lifestyle-oriented, surfers, creatives) and the need to pitch to playlist algorithms.

### Instagram (The Visual Hub)
*   **Frequency:** 3-4 posts per week (2 Reels, 1-2 Carousels).
*   **Tone:** Highly aesthetic, curated, poetic. 
*   **Bio:** "island energy, east coast grit. / maui, hi / listen to 'castaway' ↓"
*   **Unique to Platform:** Carousels work incredibly well for the "photo dump / 35mm film" aesthetic that fits the chillwave/indie pop genre.

### TikTok (The Lo-Fi Diary)
*   **Frequency:** 3-5 times a week.
*   **Tone:** Raw, unpolished, zero-hook. Let the music speak.
*   **Bio:** "maui based reggae-rock. slow down a bit."
*   **Unique to Platform:** "POV" videos. Example: *POV: you skipped work to drive up the coast* with the *North Side* audio playing over grainy dashboard footage. TikTok rewards the "bedroom pop" aesthetic.

### YouTube (The Live Room)
*   **Frequency:** 1x a month.
*   **Tone:** Professional but intimate.
*   **Unique to Platform:** "Live from the Porch" sessions. Since the mix of *North Side* was noted as muddy, providing crisp, live acoustic sessions on YouTube gives audiophiles a place to really hear the lyrics and musicianship.

## 10. APPENDIX: WHY THESE DECISIONS
> **Traces back to:** The core requirement to derive every strategy strictly from the musical DNA.

1.  **The Decision:** The Deep Ocean Blue & Terracotta color palette.
    *   *Musical Evidence:* The heavy, warm bassline mixed with the bright, analog acoustic guitar strumming.
    *   *Why it matters:* It visually communicates the exact sonic balance of the band—deep groove mixed with organic warmth—before the listener even hits play.
2.  **The Decision:** Lowercase, short-sentence copywriting.
    *   *Musical Evidence:* The mid-range, conversational vocal delivery and the 86-100 BPM tempos.
    *   *Why it matters:* High-energy copywriting creates a cognitive dissonance with chillwave/reggae-rock music. The copy must "breathe" at the same speed as the songs.
3.  **The Decision:** Emulating 35mm film and Kodak Portra 400 grading.
    *   *Musical Evidence:* The "muddy," hazy, lo-fi bedroom pop mix of *North Side* and the vintage spring reverb in *Castaway*.
    *   *Why it matters:* The music is intentionally unpolished and nostalgic. Crisp, hyper-modern digital photography would alienate the indie/surf demographic that connects with this specific sound.
4.  **The Decision:** The "Unplugged Philosophy" content pillar.
    *   *Musical Evidence:* The foley of the crickets, the lyrical theme of the "south side/north side" journey, and the title *Castaway*.
    *   *Why it matters:* Fans of this genre aren't just buying music; they are buying an aspirational lifestyle of freedom and relaxation. The band must embody this lifestyle visually.
5.  **The Decision:** Banning hype emojis (🔥🚨) and "stream now" language.
    *   *Musical Evidence:* The 4/10 and 5/10 energy levels noted in the A&R report. The music is a slow burn.
    *   *Why it matters:* The band's whole ethos is rejecting the societal "grind." If their marketing feels like a desperate social media hustle, they lose all authenticity with their target audience.
